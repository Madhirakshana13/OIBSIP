# Social Engineering Attacks

## Introduction

Social engineering is a type of cyberattack that manipulates people into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise security. Instead of targeting technical vulnerabilities, attackers exploit human psychology.

---

# 1. Phishing

## Description

Phishing attacks use fraudulent emails, messages, or websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal information.

## Impact

- Identity theft
- Financial losses
- Unauthorized account access
- Data breaches

## Real-World Example

In 2016, attackers used phishing emails to compromise employee credentials at several organizations, leading to significant data breaches.

## Prevention

- Verify email senders
- Avoid suspicious links
- Enable multi-factor authentication
- Conduct security awareness training

---

# 2. Pretexting

## Description

Pretexting occurs when an attacker creates a fabricated scenario to gain a victim's trust and obtain confidential information.

## Example

An attacker may impersonate a bank employee and request account details for "verification purposes."

## Impact

- Information disclosure
- Financial fraud
- Identity theft

## Prevention

- Verify identities before sharing information
- Follow organizational verification procedures
- Limit disclosure of sensitive information

---

# 3. Baiting

## Description

Baiting involves offering something enticing to lure victims into compromising their security.

## Example

An attacker leaves an infected USB drive in a public place hoping someone will connect it to a computer.

## Impact

- Malware infections
- Data theft
- System compromise

## Prevention

- Avoid unknown USB devices
- Use endpoint protection software
- Educate users about social engineering tactics

---

# Case Studies

## Google and Facebook Scam (2013–2015)

A fraudster impersonated a legitimate supplier and tricked employees into making fraudulent payments, resulting in losses exceeding $100 million.

## COVID-19 Phishing Campaigns

Cybercriminals exploited public fear during the pandemic by sending fake health-related emails containing malicious links.

---

# Recommendations

1. Conduct regular security awareness training.
2. Use multi-factor authentication.
3. Verify requests for sensitive information.
4. Keep systems and software updated.
5. Report suspicious communications immediately.

---

# Conclusion

Social engineering attacks remain one of the most effective cyber threats because they target human behavior. Organizations and individuals must remain vigilant and implement security best practices to reduce risks.
